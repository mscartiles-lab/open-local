Local Marketplace Platform
MVP Product Requirements Document
Tagline
Shop Local Wherever You Are
 
1. Product Vision
The platform is a hyper-local digital marketplace designed to connect farmers market vendors, local producers, and small-batch artisans directly with consumers across Florida.
The goal of the platform is to allow consumers to discover and purchase local products anytime, not just during farmers market hours.
At the same time, the platform provides vendors with an affordable digital storefront that allows them to sell inventory between market days and prevent product spoilage.
The platform functions as a local discovery engine, helping consumers find nearby vendors and products in real time.
 
2. Problem Being Solved
Local vendors face several structural challenges:
Limited Selling Hours
Farmers markets operate only certain days of the week and limited hours.
Perishable Inventory
Many vendors sell food products that spoil quickly. Unsold inventory often results in waste.
Lack of Digital Infrastructure
Small vendors often lack:
• websites
• online ordering systems
• marketing tools
• customer discovery outside market days
Consumer Access
Consumers increasingly want to support local businesses, but:
• markets are not always open
• vendors are difficult to locate outside of market days
 
3. Platform Solution
The platform creates a statewide local marketplace across Florida where:
Consumers can:
• discover nearby vendors
• purchase products directly
• reserve items for upcoming markets
• support local producers anytime
Vendors gain:
• a digital storefront
• a marketplace discovery engine
• the ability to release inventory anytime
• tools to prevent spoilage and sell excess inventory
The platform enables consumers to truly Shop Local Wherever You Are.

5. Vendor Onboarding Flow (MVP)
Vendor onboarding should be simple but structured to create trust.
Step 1 — Account Creation
Vendor registers using:
• email or phone number
• password creation
• two-factor authentication
 
Step 2 — Vendor Setup
Vendor provides:
• business / brand name
• vendor category
• location
• product types
Optional information may include:
• markets they sell at
• certifications
• social media links
 
Step 3 — Vendor Storefront Creation
Each vendor receives a public storefront page that includes:
• vendor bio
• photos
• product listings
• ratings and reviews
• optional market schedule
 
Step 4 — Payment Setup
Vendor connects a payout method.
Platform payments are processed through a secure payment gateway with escrow functionality.
Funds are released after transaction completion.
 
6. Consumer Onboarding
Consumer onboarding introduces the platform concept.
Three onboarding screens:
1. Discover local vendors near you
2. Buy fresh products anytime
3. Shop Local Wherever You Are
Consumers can optionally select interests such as:
• fresh food
• baked goods
• plants
• artisan goods
This helps personalize their discovery feed.
 
7. Core Marketplace Features (MVP)
Local Discovery Engine
The platform automatically shows products and vendors near the user based on location.
Discovery feed includes:
• nearby vendors
• new product listings
• featured vendors
• promoted listings
 
Home Screen Feature: Local Near Me Now
The home screen displays products currently available nearby.
Examples:
• bread baked today
• honey available nearby
• fresh produce available
• small-batch food items
This creates a real-time local marketplace.
 
Vendor Storefront Pages
Each vendor has a dedicated profile page acting as a mini online store.
Vendor pages include:
• vendor bio
• product catalog
• photos
• customer reviews
 
Batch Drops
Vendors can release small-batch inventory when products become available.
Example:
“Fresh sourdough batch available.”
Nearby users can receive notifications.
This feature encourages real-time purchasing.
 
Spoilage Rescue Listings
Vendors can mark products as discounted surplus inventory.
Examples:
• leftover produce after market
• extra baked goods
• surplus prepared food
Listings may appear as:
Market Surplus – Discounted
This feature helps reduce food waste and encourages quick sales.
 
Pre-Orders for Market Pickup
Consumers can reserve items before market day.
Example reservations:
• bread
• produce
• prepared food
The vendor prepares the quantity needed.
This reduces unsold inventory and improves vendor planning.
 
8. Messaging and Communication

9. Trust and Security
Security features include:
• two-factor authentication
• escrow-based payments
• vendor ratings and reviews
• transaction history
• verified vendor badges (future phase)
 
10. Monetization Model
The platform generates revenue through several channels.
 
Buyer Processing Fee
A small processing fee is added to each purchase.
Example range:
3–5% of transaction value.
 
Vendor Subscription
Vendors receive a 45-day free trial.
After the trial period, vendors pay a monthly subscription to maintain their storefront and access marketplace tools.
 
Boosted Listings
Vendors can pay to increase visibility of products within the discovery feed.
 
Brand Advertising
Local and regional businesses may advertise on the platform.
Examples:
• kitchen supply companies
• packaging suppliers
• farm supply companies
• local service providers
Ad placements include:
• home screen banners
• featured sections
• sponsored vendor placements
